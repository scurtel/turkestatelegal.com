Buying property in Turkey can be a solid investment and a lifestyle decision, but it is important to follow a careful legal process from the start.

## 1) Confirm foreign ownership eligibility

Not all locations and property types are available under the same rules. Before paying a deposit, verify that a foreign buyer can legally purchase that specific property.

## 2) Verify title deed details

Ask for a copy of the title deed information and confirm:

- The legal owner matches the seller
- The property type and independent section details are correct
- There are no unexpected restrictions that block transfer

## 3) Check debts and legal risks

A property can carry debts, taxes, or utility obligations. A proper legal review should confirm what must be cleared before transfer.

## 4) Use a clear purchase contract

Your contract should define:

- Payment schedule and currency
- Deadlines and transfer conditions
- Penalties if either side fails to perform
- Responsibility for taxes and transfer fees

## 5) Complete transfer officially

The transfer should be completed through official channels at the title deed office. Keep all payment records and signed documents in a secure folder.

## Final note

A calm, document-first approach reduces risk. Even when the process seems straightforward, a focused legal review in English can prevent expensive mistakes.
