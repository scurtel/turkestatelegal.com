# How to Make a Land Registry Appointment in Turkey as a Foreign Buyer

For many foreign buyers, the land registry appointment is the most stressful part of the purchase. Not because it is impossible, but because it is highly procedural. Even when the property is suitable and both sides agree on price, the transfer can stall if appointment preparation is incomplete.

The appointment stage is where legal checks, payment planning, identity documents, representation authority, and office logistics all converge. A small mismatch discovered at this point can delay completion or require rescheduling.

This guide explains the practical appointment logic, what to prepare beforehand, where coordination usually fails, and how legal support can reduce friction. Procedural details can vary by office practice and over time, so always confirm current requirements before your transfer date.

## What the Land Registry Appointment Actually Does

The appointment is the formal transfer stage where title registration is completed (assuming all legal conditions are satisfied). It is not a casual meeting and not just an administrative formality.

In practical terms, it is the checkpoint where:

- buyer and seller identity/authority are validated
- transaction documents are confirmed
- transfer declarations are processed
- title registration is finalized in the official system

## Step 1: Prepare the File Before Requesting the Appointment

Many delays happen because parties request an appointment too early, then try to fix missing items under time pressure.

### Confirm Personal and Representation Documents

If you attend in person, ensure passport and supporting identity documents are valid and consistent across the file.  
If someone attends for you, ensure power of attorney wording is precise, valid, and aligned with the specific transfer actions required.

### Confirm Property and Seller Readiness

Before appointment scheduling, confirm that the property is transfer-ready and the seller has clean authority to transfer.  
This is especially important if prior encumbrance or debt clearance steps were required.

### Confirm Payment Method and Evidence Protocol

Payment planning must be coordinated with transfer timing. If payment is structured around transfer-day events, everyone should know the sequence and evidence requirements in advance.

## Step 2: Understand Appointment Logic and Timing

Land registry workflows typically operate on scheduled slots and document-controlled sequencing. Missing a slot can mean delay, especially in busy periods.

### Why Timing Control Matters

- translation/notarization may take longer than expected
- bank transfer windows can affect same-day coordination
- cross-border buyers may face travel or time-zone constraints

A realistic timeline with buffer is usually safer than aggressive “same-week completion” plans.

### Who Should Coordinate?

In foreign-buyer files, one coordinator should manage communications across:

- buyer
- seller
- legal representatives
- translator/interpreter where needed
- bank/payment stakeholders

Without clear coordination, parties often discover contradictory assumptions on appointment day.

## Step 3: Appointment-Day Preparation Checklist

### Identity and Authority

- valid personal identity documents
- any required translated/notarized documents
- valid power of attorney documents (if applicable)

### Transaction Documents

- final agreed transfer details
- proof that preconditions were satisfied
- evidence related to payment structure as required

### Logistics

- confirmed appointment time and location
- interpreter support if language assistance is needed
- contingency plan if a document issue appears

This stage is not the right time to resolve major unresolved legal questions. Those should be closed before arrival.

## Common Coordination Failures (and How to Prevent Them)

### Failure 1: Last-Minute Document Mismatch

A spelling inconsistency or missing certification can trigger rescheduling.  
Prevention: run a final document consistency check 24-48 hours in advance.

### Failure 2: Unclear Payment Sequence

If buyer and seller are not aligned on when funds move relative to registration events, tension rises quickly.  
Prevention: agree in writing on payment milestones and documentary proof points.

### Failure 3: Incomplete POA Scope

A representative may attend with authority that is too narrow for required actions.  
Prevention: legal review of POA language against the exact transfer scenario.

### Failure 4: Assuming Earlier Checks Are Still Current

A check completed weeks earlier may not reflect current registry status.  
Prevention: final pre-transfer confirmation shortly before appointment.

## Language and Interpretation Considerations

Foreign buyers unfamiliar with Turkish procedures often underestimate interpretation needs. Even if the process appears routine, misunderstanding one declaration can create avoidable legal and practical confusion.

Best practice is to arrange professional language support where needed and ensure all key legal and financial points are understood before signing or confirming final statements.

## What Happens If the Appointment Must Be Rescheduled?

Rescheduling is not always a disaster, but it can create:

- additional logistics costs
- contract timing pressure
- tension between parties on responsibility for delay

To reduce impact, contracts should ideally address deadline handling and responsibility allocation for preventable delays.

## How Legal Guidance Improves Appointment Success Rates

Legal support at this stage is not only about attendance. It is about reducing avoidable failure points before the slot is booked.

A practical legal workflow can include:

- pre-appointment readiness review
- confirmation of authority and document validity
- coordination of payment and transfer sequence
- issue-handling protocol if a problem appears on the day

This is often where foreign buyers save the most time: not by rushing the appointment, but by making the appointment predictable.

## FAQ

### Can I make a land registry appointment before all documents are ready?

You can attempt to schedule, but it is usually better to finalize core readiness first. Incomplete files often lead to rescheduling and avoidable delay.

### Do I have to attend in person?

Not always. In some cases, a properly authorized representative can act for you. Power of attorney scope and document quality are critical in these scenarios.

### Is one appointment enough for every transaction?

Many straightforward files complete in one slot, but this depends on document completeness, payment coordination, and whether unexpected issues appear.

### Do language barriers matter at appointment stage?

Yes. Misunderstanding legal or financial statements can create risk. Professional interpretation support is often advisable for non-Turkish speakers.

### What is the biggest practical mistake at appointment stage?

Treating appointment booking as the goal, instead of treating transfer readiness as the goal. The slot itself is only useful if your file is genuinely ready.
